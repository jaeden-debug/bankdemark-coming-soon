# Blackwater Aquatics Betta Content Case Study

## Starting Point

Blackwater Aquatics had real betta breeding experience and a product ecosystem connected to betta care, fry feeding, live foods, and aquarium health.

The opportunity was to use betta informational content to attract high-intent searchers and guide them toward live food products.

## Problem

Betta searches are competitive.

Broad terms like “betta fish care” are difficult.

The better opportunity was symptom-based and problem-based long-tail content:

- betta fish not eating
- sick betta fish
- betta fish hiding
- betta fish bloated
- betta fish laying on bottom
- betta fin rot
- betta fry food

## Strategy

The content strategy targeted urgent owner problems.

Each article connected symptoms back to practical causes:

- poor water quality
- stress
- temperature issues
- poor diet
- constipation
- illness
- weak appetite
- recent tank changes

Then the content naturally introduced live food solutions where relevant:

- scuds
- daphnia
- microworms
- baby brine shrimp

## Results

The betta content began generating impressions for long-tail symptom queries.

Notably, “betta fish not eating” appeared in Search Console with meaningful impressions.

This confirmed that problem-solving content could open new topical doors while supporting product discovery.

## Operator Observation

Urgent pet-care searches convert differently.

The user is not casually browsing.

They are worried, searching fast, and looking for a trusted answer.

That makes clarity, honesty, and practical next steps more important than aggressive selling.
