# Blackwater Aquatics SEO Growth Case Study

## Starting Point

Blackwater Aquatics Canada is a niche ecommerce brand selling live aquarium foods, scuds, daphnia, bettas, shrimp, and live cultures in Canada.

The site started with low domain authority, limited backlinks, and a small but highly specific product catalog.

The strongest commercial opportunity was live scuds because Canadian search competition was weak, but the site lacked a mature SEO architecture.

## Problem

The main problems were:

- weak topical authority
- thin product and collection content
- inconsistent metadata
- limited internal linking
- poor image naming and alt text
- few trust signals
- few supporting articles around live foods
- limited content depth around scuds and daphnia

The site had products, but Google did not yet have enough supporting context to treat Blackwater Aquatics as a live food authority.

## Strategy

The strategy focused on topical saturation around live foods.

Actions included:

- building scud-focused product content
- creating live scud informational articles
- targeting “live scuds Canada” and “scuds for sale” intent
- adding FAQ sections
- creating daphnia comparison content
- optimizing image filenames and alt text
- publishing betta illness and live food support articles
- internally linking informational pages back to scuds and daphnia products

## Results

Observed results included:

- scud product page becoming the strongest organic landing page
- GSC impressions expanding across long-tail aquarium queries
- rankings appearing for “scuds for sale,” “live scuds for sale,” and related terms
- informational pages beginning to support product discovery
- increased visibility for betta illness queries
- stronger topical relationship between bettas, live food, scuds, daphnia, and fry feeding

## Operator Observation

The biggest wins did not come from backlinks.

They came from making Google understand the niche.

Once the site had enough semantically connected content around live fish food, scuds, daphnia, fry care, and betta feeding, the product pages had far more context behind them.
