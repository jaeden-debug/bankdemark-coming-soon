# BankDeMark Editorial Voice System

BankDeMark content should sound like experienced operators explaining real-world systems.

Not polished corporate copy.

Not generic AI summaries.

## Core Voice Rules

### 1. Use Punches

Short blunt statements create rhythm.

Example:

Most ecommerce stores completely ignore collection pages.

That mistake alone can suppress rankings for years.

## 2. Use Contrarian Observations

Avoid generic advice.

Example:

Publishing more content is not automatically an SEO strategy.

Publishing interconnected content is.

## 3. Use Operator Language

Write like someone who has actually built things.

Example:

Google detects topical chaos surprisingly fast once a site scales beyond 100 articles.

## 4. Break Perfect Rhythm

Avoid overly balanced paragraph structures.

Mix:

- short paragraphs
- long explanations
- abrupt transitions
- occasional sharp opinions

## 5. Avoid Generic AI Tone

Avoid phrases like:

- "in today's digital landscape"
- "it is important to note"
- "businesses should leverage"
- "unlock the power"
- "delve into"

## 6. Use Real Observations

Best content often includes:

- mistakes
- failed experiments
- unexpected outcomes
- operational bottlenecks
- tradeoffs

## 7. Sound Certain When Appropriate

Weak:
"This may potentially help rankings."

Better:
"Thin collection pages are one of the biggest silent SEO killers in ecommerce."

## 8. Use Semantic Repetition Carefully

Repeat major entities intentionally:

- topical authority
- search intent
- internal linking
- collection pages
- organic assets
- EEAT
- content clusters

This reinforces entity ownership.

## 9. Every Article Needs:

- at least 1 operator observation
- at least 1 contrarian statement
- at least 1 proof section
- at least 1 framework reference
- at least 1 internal authority link

## 10. Final Rule

The goal is not to sound like AI.

The goal is to sound like the smartest operator in the room.
